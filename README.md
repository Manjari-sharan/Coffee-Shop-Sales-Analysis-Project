# Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analysis-Project
Unlocking Insights from Coffee Shop Sales Data Using Python Data Analytics Packages

# Tools Used
Python Data Analytics packages : Numpy, Pandas

Python Visualization packages : Matplotlib, Seaborn

# Objectives
<b>Understand Sales Trends:</b> To analyse sales performance based on transaction date, time, and store location to identify peak periods and low-demand times.

<b>Product Category Analysis:</b> To evaluate the performance of different product categories and types to identify high-revenue and low-performing items.

<b>Customer Traffic Insights:</b> To determine the busiest hours and days to optimize staffing and inventory management.

<b>Enhance Customer Experience:</b> To identify the most popular products and transaction times to better cater to customer preferences and streamline operations

# Key Insights

📊 Overall Transaction Overview

- All sales were recorded in 2023, covering a six-month analysis period.
- Customer transactions were primarily low-basket purchases, with single-item transactions being the most common, followed by two-item baskets.
- Sales activity was concentrated in the morning and daytime hours (8 AM – 11 AM range), indicating strong commuter and early-day demand.

🏬 Store Performance

- All three store locations performed relatively consistently, showing a balanced revenue distribution across stores.
- The most noticeable performance gap was observed at Hell’s Kitchen, which showed slightly stronger sales contribution compared to other locations.
- This suggests location-based variation in customer traffic, but no extreme underperforming store.
  
📈 Sales Trends (Time & Seasonality)

- Monthly trend:
   - Sales were strongest in the middle of the observed period, with June emerging as the peak month.
  
- Day-level performance:
   - Monday recorded the highest sales ($101,677), indicating strong weekday demand.
   - Saturday had the lowest sales ($96,894), showing relatively weaker weekend performance compared to expectations.
     
- Time-of-day analysis:
   - Morning hours contributed the highest revenue ($388,289).
   - Evening hours were the lowest-performing segment ($146,926), highlighting a sharp decline in late-day purchases.
     
🧾 Product & Category Performance

- Coffee was the dominant category, driving the highest overall revenue.
- Tea also showed strong performance, confirming it as a key complementary category.
- The best-selling product overall was Barista Espresso ($91,406), making it the top revenue generator.
- In terms of volume, Brewed Chai Tea led unit sales, indicating strong customer preference for tea-based beverages.
  
🛍️ Category-Level Product Leaders

- Bakery: Scone was the top-selling item
- Branded: Housewares led sales
- Coffee: Gourmet brewed coffee was the best performer
- Coffee Beans: Organic Beans were the top seller
- Flavours: Regular syrup dominated sales
- Loose Tea: Chai Tea led category performance
- Packaged Chocolate: Drinking Chocolate was highest selling
- Tea: Brewed Chai Tea was the top product
- Drinking Chocolate category: Only consisted of Hot Chocolate products
  
📉 Low-Performing Products

- Green Beans (Coffee Beans category) was the lowest-performing product, generating only $1,340 in sales.
This indicates a potential inventory optimization opportunity or weak customer demand for raw bean products.

💡 Business Takeaways

- Strong morning-driven demand suggests optimizing staffing and inventory before peak hours.
- Coffee and Tea categories should remain the core revenue drivers, with continued focus on high-performing SKUs like Espresso and Chai Tea.
- Low-performing products (e.g., Green Beans) may require repositioning, bundling, or discontinuation.
- Store performance consistency indicates opportunities for standardized operational improvements rather than location-specific fixes.
